**Section H: Issues for your legal advisers**

We are not legally qualified, and we strongly recommend that you obtain independent legal advice before committing to this purchase. 

The following matters should be brought to the attention of your legal adviser as a matter of urgency. As noted in the body of this report, the inspection was significantly restricted, and several elements were not accessible or not present at the time of our visit (D1 through G5). Consequently, the burden of verifying the physical integrity and regulatory compliance of the property falls heavily upon the legal due diligence process.

**Regulations, Planning and Building Control**
Your legal adviser should verify whether any extensions, structural alterations, or conversions have been carried out at the property. Under the **Town and Country Planning Act 1990** and the **Building Regulations**, any significant works require formal approval. Since we were unable to inspect the internal roof structure (E1), main walls (D4), or partitions (E3), we cannot confirm if structural modifications have been executed. Your solicitor must request all relevant Completion Certificates and Planning Consents. Failure to obtain these could lead to enforcement action or issues with the **Defective Premises Act 1972 (as amended 2023)**, particularly if works were not performed in a "workmanlike manner" or with "proper materials," potentially rendering the dwelling unfit for habitation.

  <span class="section-code">M</span>
  <h1>Description of the RICS Home Survey — Level 3</h1>
</div>

The RICS Home Survey – Level 3 (formerly the RICS Building Survey) is a comprehensive inspection of the property. The surveyor will carry out a thorough inspection of the property, both inside and outside, reporting on a wider range of issues than the Level 1 or Level 2 reports.

The Level 3 report is suited to all residential properties, and is particularly appropriate for:
- Older properties
- Properties that have been altered or extended
- Properties where you plan to carry out major renovation or alteration
- Unusual properties, or those constructed with uncommon materials



**What the inspection covers:**

The surveyor inspects the inside and outside of the main building and all permanent outbuildings, recording the construction and defects that are evident. This includes woodwork and other parts of the structure that are exposed or can be seen. Parts of the electricity, gas/oil, water, heating and drainage services that can be seen are inspected. The surveyor does not test the services. The surveyor notes any damage to the property that may have been caused by the presence of asbestos.

**What the inspection does NOT cover:**

Concealed areas or those obstructed by furniture are not inspected. The surveyor does not:'
- Move furniture, lift floor coverings or carry out invasive testing
- Assess specialist installations (swimming pools, lifts, etc.)
- Carry out formal valuations or reinstatement cost assessments
- Provide legal/conveyancing advice


### Condition rating definitions

**Condition rating 1 — Green:** No repair is currently needed. Normal maintenance must be carried out.

**Condition rating 2 — Amber:** Defects that need repairing or replacing but are not considered to be either serious or urgent. The property must be maintained in the normal way.

**Condition rating 3 — Red:** Defects that are serious and/or need to be repaired, replaced or investigated urgently.

**NI — Not inspected:** The element was not inspected and the reasons are given.


The report is prepared by a qualified RICS member in accordance with the RICS Home Survey Standard (effective 1 February 2024). The surveyor's liability for any negligence is limited to the fee paid for the report unless otherwise agreed in writing. This report is for the use of the named client only and no liability is accepted to any third party.

**Complaints handling:** In the event of any dispute, the complaints-handling procedure as approved by RICS will be followed. Details can be obtained from the surveyor's office or from RICS directly.
